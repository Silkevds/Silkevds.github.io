More often than not, my meat paintings are made exclusively from observation. Usually one or multiple pieces of meat are hung up on the wall, which creates a composition. The purpose is to rip meat out of its usual context. We see it in warehouses, on our plates, at the butchershops and many other places. It's at those places where we don't think twice about what we're seeing. But when suddenly we see this red, bloody piece of flesh hung up on the wall, it creates a very strange experience. It's this experience I'm trying to capture. To think twice about what we're consuming and to see the beauty in it, or perhaps exactly the opposite.
